Design Portfolio Sophie Vryenhoek

A portfolio designed by Sophie Vryenhoek. She spent most of her childhood in Queenstown, which sparked her passion for appreciating beauty. Her work is motivated by improving user experiences and interactions between people and objects, apps, and products. Her focus is on UX design; however, she adds her own flare to her designs through graphic design and photography. Sophie’s designs are driven by making a positive social change in existing systems. She is fascinated by colour and bold graphic imagery, which is evident throughout her designs. Sophie is currently studying for a Bachelor of Design and Bachelor of Commerce full-time at Victoria University. Her majors include Interaction design and marketing and she has thus far completed two years of study. While she has a couple of years of study left, she has started to branch out with her own personal endeavours and has many goals for the future. .

Passion for the Envioronment

What is low carbon web design and why is it important? Digital information is stored in vast data centres that use electricity at huge rates. Although we can't physically see the impact of the internet on the environment, the average website produces over three grams of CO2 per pageview. By removing all the unnecessary data, each website's carbon footprint could be significantly reduced.

Display

Elements of this website have been designed with the intention of reducing it's energy use. A black background consumes less energy than with a white background, so I have designed the colour pallet of my website to achieve a low impact web design.

Reduce

I could however further mitigate my website's carbon footprint by dithering images and using even more reduced sizing, with less colour and lower resolution. However as a portfolio I though this was an aspect that couldn't be completely changed.

Renewable

A company hosts webpages using electricity. Once I learn this skill I will ensure to use a hosting company who focussed on green renewable energy, this would also ensure the webpage avoids polluting and generating CO2 emissions.
